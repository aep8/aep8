Temas de Investigación

Proyectos de Investigación

(En inglés)

Gender and Racial Bias in Fact-Checking Journalism

Do race and gender influence the coverage decisions of fact-checking agencies? Previous studies suggest that politicians from underrepresented groups tend to receive less coverage in traditional media, but no research has explored this possibility in fact-checking journalism. Fact-checkers’ conceptualization of newsworthiness might inadvertently perpetuate existing racial disparities in media coverage, which can have important consequences for the racial gap in informational resources and political accountability.

A Dos Voces: How News Source and Language Impact Candidate Evaluations

(With Kishan Bhakta) This project examines how differences in narratives from English-speaking and Spanish-speaking media outlets impact public attitudes toward candidates among Latino audiences. It employs a mixed methods approach that combines descriptive evidence from a novel content analysis with experimental evidence in the form of a conjoint design.

Campaign Negativity and Political Participation in the Social Media Era

(With Asahi Obata) Do negative political campaigns lead to lower political participation? We revisit this question in the current era of multiple social and media platforms. While several studies have explored whether negative campaigns on traditional media lead to demobilization, we argue that this relationship is conditional on the media voters see negative campaigning on. We expect a demobilization effect to be larger when negative campaigning is fielded on a platform perceived as positive versus one where subjects already expect to see negative content.
